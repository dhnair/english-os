# Answer Key: Day 8 Information Framing

**1.**
* **The Logic:** Shift the Object ("the issue") to the Subject slot and use `was + V3` (Past Simple Passive).
* **Clean Code:** "The QA team found a critical bug, so the issue **was escalated** to the lead developer."

**2.**
* **The Logic:** Identify the Doer ("my sister") and move her to the Subject slot. Revert to Past Simple Active.
* **Clean Code:** "My sister **whipped up** a delicious, spicy brunch in under ten minutes."

**3.**
* **The Logic:** Remove the Doer ("we/our team") entirely to hide the blame. Focus on the objects ("a mistake", "the timeline"). 
* **Clean Code:** "A mistake **was made**, and the timeline **was delayed**."

**4.**
* **The Logic:** The Doer ("an old friend") moves to the front. This fixes the clunky, unnatural phrasing of a passive casual interaction.
* **Clean Code:** "An old friend from university **caught up with** me during my evening walk."

**5.**
* **The Logic:** Remove the junior devs from the logs to protect them. Shift the focus to the code using Past Simple Passive.
* **Clean Code:** "The broken code **was pushed to production**."

**6.**
* **The Logic:** Move the Object ("the final build") to the front. You can drop the team completely or leave them at the end with "by".
* **Clean Code:** "The final build **was pushed to production** on Friday."

**7.**
* **The Logic:** The Doer ("my brother") moves to the front. Shift the verb back to Past Simple Active.
* **Clean Code:** "My brother **whipped up** a quick, spicy meal before he left."

**8.**
* **The Logic:** Protect your freelance reputation. Remove "I" and state the broken status neutrally using Past Simple Passive.
* **Clean Code:** "The payment gateway **was broken**."

**9.**
* **The Logic:** The UX of the original passive sentence is terrible for casual conversation. The Doer ("I") must take the Subject slot.
* **Clean Code:** "I **caught up with** an old colleague during my post-dinner walk."

**10.**
* **The Logic:** Shift "the issue" to the front of the second clause. 
* **Clean Code:** "Because the workstation kept crashing, the issue **was escalated** to IT support."

**11.**
* **The Logic:** Shift the Object ("a quick batch of overnight oats with chia seeds") to the front.
* **Clean Code:** "A quick batch of overnight oats with chia seeds **was whipped up** for brunch."

**12.**
* **The Logic:** Identify the Doer ("the junior contractor") and put them in the driver's seat.
* **Clean Code:** "The junior contractor **pushed** the untested database migration to production."

**13.**
* **The Logic:** This will sound incredibly clunky, but mechanically it compiles. "My sister" becomes the Subject.
* **Clean Code:** "My sister **was caught up with** over the phone to discuss her English lessons."

**14.**
* **The Logic:** Remove the client from the sentence to focus purely on the action that occurred.
* **Clean Code:** "The issue **was escalated** immediately after the server went offline."

**15.**
* **The Logic:** De-escalate the tension by removing "Your team" from the error report. 
* **Clean Code:** "The wrong update **was pushed to production**."

**16.**
* **The Logic:** Inject yourself as the Doer to make the code Active.
* **Clean Code:** "I **whipped up** some extra spicy noodles for dinner."

**17.**
* **The Logic:** Modals never change their shape. Add the base "be" + V3. 
* **Clean Code:** "The issue **must be escalated** before the whole system fails."

**18.**
* **The Logic:** This is another "Clunky UX" test. It compiles, but a native speaker would rarely use the passive voice for this.
* **Clean Code:** "My family **is usually caught up with** when I walk around the neighborhood."

**19.**
* **The Logic:** The Doer ("the lead Python developer") takes the front. The `will be pushed` passive shifts to the `will push` active.
* **Clean Code:** "The lead Python developer **will push** the critical security patch to production."

**20.**
* **The Logic:** Remove the pronoun "You" entirely. Focus on the code and the errors so the critique is objective, not personal.
* **Clean Code:** "Terrible code **was written**," OR "A lot of errors **were made** in this build."
